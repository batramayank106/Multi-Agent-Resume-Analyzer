Recruiter Handbook: Evaluating Resumes Effectively

1. Initial Screening Criteria:
   - Overall presentation and professionalism (5-10 seconds first impression).
   - Relevance of experience to the role.
   - Presence of required skills and qualifications.
   - Career progression and stability.
   - Education and certifications.

2. Red Flags:
   - Employment gaps of 6+ months without explanation.
   - Frequent job changes (less than 1 year per role).
   - Typos, grammatical errors, or inconsistent formatting.
   - Generic, untailored resumes.
   - Exaggerated or vague descriptions.
   - Missing contact information.

3. Green Flags:
   - Clear career progression with increasing responsibility.
   - Quantifiable achievements and metrics.
   - Relevant certifications and continuous learning.
   - Leadership experience and team collaboration.
   - Strong action verbs and specific examples.

4. Evaluating Readability:
   - Clear section headings and logical flow.
   - Consistent formatting throughout.
   - Appropriate use of white space.
   - Bullet points are concise and impactful.
   - Font size is readable (10-12pt).

5. Evaluating Impact:
   - Does the resume show results, not just responsibilities?
   - Are achievements quantified with numbers?
   - Does the candidate demonstrate initiative?
   - Are there examples of problem-solving?

6. Evaluating Leadership:
   - Team leadership or mentorship experience.
   - Project management or ownership.
   - Cross-functional collaboration examples.
   - Decision-making responsibilities.

7. Evaluating Communication:
   - Clear and professional language.
   - Technical concepts explained effectively.
   - Appropriate tone and terminology.

8. Evaluating Achievement Orientation:
   - Consistent pattern of exceeding expectations.
   - Awards, recognition, or promotions.
   - Side projects and continuous learning.
   - Contributions to the broader community.

9. Pass/Reject Criteria:
   - PASS: Candidate meets 70%+ of requirements with strong indicators.
   - REJECT: Below 50% match or multiple red flags.
   - Confidence rating: 0-100 based on evidence strength.
