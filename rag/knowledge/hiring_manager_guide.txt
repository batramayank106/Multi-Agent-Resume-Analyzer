Hiring Manager Evaluation Guide: Technical Depth and Project Quality

1. Technical Depth Assessment:
   - Evaluate proficiency in required programming languages.
   - Assess understanding of system design and architecture.
   - Look for experience with relevant frameworks and tools.
   - Consider depth vs. breadth of technical knowledge.
   - Evaluate problem-solving approach and analytical thinking.

2. Project Quality Evaluation:
   - Complexity and scope of projects undertaken.
   - Technologies and architectures used.
   - Whether the project solved a real business problem.
   - Evidence of following software engineering best practices.
   - Team size and the candidate's specific role.

3. Internship Readiness:
   - Basic proficiency in core technologies.
   - Willingness to learn and adapt.
   - Strong foundational knowledge.
   - Portfolio of academic or personal projects.
   - Communication and collaboration skills.

4. Domain Fit:
   - Relevant industry experience.
   - Understanding of domain-specific challenges.
   - Familiarity with domain tools and practices.
   - Ability to contribute from day one.

5. Technical Interview Focus Areas:
   - Data structures and algorithms.
   - System design and scalability.
   - Code quality and testing practices.
   - Debugging and troubleshooting skills.
   - Version control and CI/CD knowledge.

6. Project Portfolio Assessment:
   - Quality over quantity (2-3 strong projects).
   - Clear problem statement and solution.
   - Appropriate technology choices.
   - Evidence of testing and documentation.
   - Deployment and real-world usage.

7. Code Review Criteria:
   - Code organization and structure.
   - Naming conventions and readability.
   - Error handling and edge cases.
   - Performance considerations.
   - Testing coverage.
