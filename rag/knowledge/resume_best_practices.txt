Resume Best Practices and Writing Guide

1. Professional Summary:
   - Write 2-3 sentences highlighting your most relevant experience.
   - Tailor to the specific role you're applying for.
   - Include years of experience, key skills, and career highlights.

2. Experience Section:
   - Use strong action verbs: "Developed", "Led", "Implemented", "Optimized".
   - Start each bullet with a different action verb.
   - Quantify results: "Increased revenue by 20%", "Reduced latency by 40%".
   - Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).
   - Include 4-6 bullet points per role.
   - Show career progression and increasing responsibility.

3. Skills Section:
   - List 8-12 relevant skills.
   - Include a mix of hard skills and soft skills.
   - Prioritize skills mentioned in the job description.
   - Remove outdated or irrelevant skills.

4. Projects Section:
   - Include 2-3 significant projects.
   - Describe your specific contribution.
   - Mention technologies used.
   - Link to live demos or GitHub repositories if available.

5. Education Section:
   - List degree, institution, and graduation year.
   - Include GPA if above 3.5.
   - Mention relevant coursework for entry-level positions.

6. Certifications:
   - List relevant certifications with issuing body and date.
   - Prioritize active and in-demand certifications.

7. Achievements:
   - Include awards, publications, patents, and speaking engagements.
   - Highlight recognition from industry bodies.

8. Length Guidelines:
   - Entry-level: 1 page
   - Mid-level: 1-2 pages
   - Senior-level: 2 pages maximum

9. Contact Information:
   - Include: Name, Phone, Email, LinkedIn, GitHub/Portfolio.
   - Exclude: Photo, age, marital status, social security number.

10. Tailoring:
    - Customize resume for each application.
    - Mirror language from the job description.
    - Highlight most relevant experience first.
