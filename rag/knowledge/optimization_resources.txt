Resume Optimization Resources and Techniques

1. Keyword Extraction:
   - Identify key skills and technologies from job descriptions.
   - Note both required and preferred qualifications.
   - Look for repeated terms and themes.
   - Identify industry buzzwords and trending technologies.

2. Bullet Point Optimization:
   - Start with powerful action verbs.
   - Include specific metrics and results.
   - Use the format: "Action verb + Task + Result + Metric"
   - Keep bullets to 1-2 lines maximum.
   - Remove weak or generic descriptions.

3. ATS Optimization:
   - Include exact keywords from the job description.
   - Use standard section headings.
   - Avoid creative formatting.
   - Save in ATS-friendly format.
   - Test resume with an ATS simulator before submitting.

4. Content Prioritization:
   - Most relevant experience first.
   - Highlight achievements that match job requirements.
   - Remove outdated or irrelevant experience.
   - Add a "Relevant Experience" section for career changers.

5. Quantification:
   - Use numbers to demonstrate impact.
   - Examples: "Managed $500K budget", "Led team of 12", "Improved efficiency by 35%".
   - Include timeframes for context.
   - Compare against benchmarks where possible.

6. Skill Gap Analysis:
   - Compare resume skills against job requirements.
   - Identify missing critical skills.
   - Prioritize skills to acquire or highlight.
   - Consider transferable skills for gaps.

7. Impact Improvement:
   - For each role, identify the single biggest achievement.
   - Ensure achievements demonstrate business impact.
   - Link technical work to business outcomes.
   - Show progression in scope and responsibility.
