ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Guidelines and Best Practices

1. Keyword Optimization:
   - Resumes must include keywords from the job description to pass ATS filters.
   - Use exact phrases from the job posting when describing your experience.
   - Include industry-standard terminology and skill names.

2. Formatting Rules:
   - Use standard section headings: "Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Projects".
   - Avoid headers/footers as ATS may not read them.
   - Do not use tables, columns, or text boxes as ATS may jumble the content.
   - Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman) at 10-12pt.
   - Save as .docx or PDF (text-based, not scanned).

3. File Naming:
   - Use "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf" format.
   - Avoid special characters in filenames.

4. Section Requirements:
   - Include a professional summary or objective at the top.
   - List work experience in reverse chronological order.
   - Include months and years for each position.
   - Quantify achievements with numbers where possible.

5. Skills Section:
   - Group skills by category (Technical, Soft Skills, Languages).
   - List both technical and soft skills.
   - Match skill names exactly as they appear in the job description.

6. Common ATS Rejection Reasons:
   - Missing key skills from the job description.
   - Unusual section headings.
   - Complex formatting (tables, columns, graphics).
   - Lack of quantifiable achievements.
   - Typos and grammatical errors.

7. ATS Scoring Factors:
   - Keyword Match: 30% of score
   - Skill Match: 20% of score
   - Experience Match: 15% of score
   - Project Match: 15% of score
   - Achievements: 10% of score
   - Education: 5% of score
   - Formatting: 5% of score

8. Score Categories:
   - 0-40: Poor - Major revisions needed
   - 40-60: Average - Needs improvement
   - 60-75: Good - Competitive
   - 75-90: Strong - Highly competitive
   - 90-100: Exceptional - Top candidate
