Interview Preparation: Technical and Behavioral Questions Guide

1. Technical Question Categories:
   - Data Structures: Arrays, Linked Lists, Trees, Graphs, Hash Tables
   - Algorithms: Sorting, Searching, Dynamic Programming, Recursion
   - System Design: Scalability, Load Balancing, Caching, Database Design
   - Object-Oriented Design: SOLID Principles, Design Patterns
   - Language-Specific: Syntax, Best Practices, Common Pitfalls

2. Behavioral Questions (STAR Method):
   - Situation: Set the context and background.
   - Task: Describe the challenge or responsibility.
   - Action: Explain what you specifically did.
   - Result: Share the outcome and impact.

3. Common Behavioral Questions:
   - "Tell me about yourself."
   - "Describe a challenging project and how you handled it."
   - "Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned."
   - "Describe a conflict with a team member and how you resolved it."
   - "Why do you want to work here?"

4. HR Questions:
   - "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
   - "What are your salary expectations?"
   - "Why are you leaving your current role?"
   - "What are your greatest strengths and weaknesses?"

5. Resume-Based Questions:
   - Be prepared to discuss every bullet point on your resume.
   - Explain your specific contribution to each project.
   - Discuss technologies used and why they were chosen.
   - Be ready to elaborate on quantified achievements.

6. Project-Based Questions:
   - "What was the architecture of your last project?"
   - "What challenges did you face and how did you overcome them?"
   - "If you could rebuild it, what would you do differently?"
   - "How did you ensure code quality?"

7. Difficulty Levels:
   - Easy: Basic concept questions and definitions.
   - Medium: Application of concepts to common scenarios.
   - Hard: Complex problem-solving and system design.
